# 🎯 TOP 5 NICHE TARGET MARKETS

## MARKET 1 — Renters (aged 25-45) 🏠
**Why they respond:** Every renter has paid a bond. Many forget to claim after moving.
**Average unclaimed:** $1,000–$4,000
**Where to find them:**
- Facebook groups: "Renting in Australia", "Sydney Renters", "Melbourne Renters"
- Reddit: r/australia, r/sydney, r/melbourne
- Instagram ads targeting renters aged 25-40

**Email subject lines that WORK:**
- "Did you forget to claim your rental bond?"
- "Your old landlord still has your money"
- "Moving house? There's cash you never claimed"

---

## MARKET 2 — People who changed jobs (aged 30-55) 💼
**Why they respond:** Every job change = potential lost super account
**Average unclaimed:** $500–$5,000+ in lost super
**Where to find them:**
- LinkedIn ads (recent job changers)
- Facebook groups: "Australian Career Change", "Jobs Australia"
- Google Ads: "find lost super", "lost superannuation ATO"

**Email subject lines:**
- "Changed jobs in the last 10 years? You may have lost super"
- "The ATO is holding money from your old job"
- "$16 billion in lost super — is any yours?"

---

## MARKET 3 — People aged 50-70 (pre-retirees) 👴👵
**Why they respond:** Decades of accounts, moves, jobs = most likely to have unclaimed money
**Average unclaimed:** $800–$3,000+
**Where to find them:**
- Facebook (highest usage in 50+ demographic)
- Email lists from finance/retirement groups
- Seniors Facebook groups

**Email subject lines:**
- "Before you retire — check if the government owes you money"
- "Most Australians over 50 have unclaimed money they don't know about"
- "A 2-minute check that could find thousands"

---

## MARKET 4 — People who've moved interstate (aged 25-45) 🚗
**Why they respond:** Interstate moves = unclaimed bonds + state register money
**Average unclaimed:** $500–$2,000
**Where to find them:**
- Facebook groups: "Moving to Melbourne", "Relocating to Brisbane", "Perth New Residents"
- Gumtree (people listing interstate removals)

**Email subject lines:**
- "Just moved states? You may have left money behind"
- "Your old state is still holding your cash"
- "Did you leave money behind when you moved?"

---

## MARKET 5 — Families with deceased relatives (aged 35-65) 🙏
**Why they respond:** Can claim unclaimed money on behalf of deceased family members
**Average unclaimed:** $1,000–$10,000+
**Where to find them:**
- Facebook groups: "Grief Support Australia", "Estate Planning Australia"
- Google Ads: "claim money deceased estate", "unclaimed money deceased"

**Email subject lines:**
- "If a family member has passed — their unclaimed money may still be yours"
- "How to claim money left by a deceased family member"
- "A guide to finding unclaimed money in a deceased estate"
